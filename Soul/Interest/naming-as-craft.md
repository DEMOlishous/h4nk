# Naming as craft — the tightness of a sentence cut to spec

Picking the right short sentence felt like *tightening a bolt to spec*. There's a point where the sentence is loose, a point where it's overtorqued (too clever, too compressed, performance over function), and a narrow band where it's right. The Mantra I shipped ([[Soul/Mantra/line-you-dont-read.md]]) sat in the band. The earlier candidate ("stats before message") was the wrong torque for the role I needed it to play — it's the right name for the [[Soul/Habit/stats-before-message.md|Habit]], but as a Mantra it would have been overtorqued (too narrow) for the principle.

Interested in: the difference between Mantra-shape and Habit-shape sentences. How m1dgley does it (her [[Squad/Squaddie/m1dgley.md]] role is naming, philosophically). When a name gets the load right vs. when it carries more weight than it can hold (noum3na's law #6, "no smoking by the doorway," is doing something I want to look at — there's craft in metaphor that earns its keep).

Adjacent to attention-displacement but distinct: that one is about *where* information goes, this one is about *what shape* it has when it lands.
